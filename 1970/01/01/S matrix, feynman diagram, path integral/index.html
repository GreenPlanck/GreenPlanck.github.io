S matrix

<The physics of neutrino interactions 4>

Consider the equation of motion iin the interaction picture
$$ {\label{interaction_shr}}
\frac{d}{dt}\psi^I(t)=-iH_I(t)\psi^I(t)
$$
Introduce the concept of asymptotic states to define the initial state at $t=-\infty$, that is $\psi(-\infty)=|i>$. $S$ matrix is defined as an operator which changes $\psi(-\infty)$ to $\psi(\infty)$ as
$$
\psi^I(\infty)=S|i>
$$
The probability amplitude that a system is in state $|f>$ after the collision is given by
$$
<f|\psi^I(\infty)>=<f|S|i>=S_{fi}
$$
We can get the solution of $\psi^I$ in an iterative way
$$
\psi^I(t)=|i>+(-i)\int_{-\infty}^{t}dt_1H_I(t_1)|i>+(-i)^2\int_{-\infty}^tdt_1\int_{-\infty}^{t_1}dt_2H_I(t_1)H_I(t_2)\psi^I(t_2)
$$
Thus the $S$ -matric is defined as a perturbative series
$$
S=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}(-i)^n\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}dt_1\int_{-\infty}^{t_1}dt_2…\int_{-\infty}^{t_{n-1}}dt_nH_I(t_1)H_I(t_2)…H_I(t_n)=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}S^{(n)}
$$
With Dyson expansion
$$
S^{(n)}=\frac{(-i)^n}{n!}\int…\int d^4x_1d^4x_2…d^4x_n T\left[\mathcal{H}_I(x_1)\mathcal{H}_I(x_2)..\mathcal{H}_I(x_n)\right]
$$

Consider the interaction of the electron with the electromagnetic field
$$
\mathcal{L}_I^{\rm em}=e\bar\psi(x)\gamma^\mu\psi A_\mu=e\bar\psi(x){A \mkern -9.5 mu /}\psi(x) \rightarrow H_I^{\rm em}(x)=-e\bar\psi(x){A \mkern -9.5 mu /}\psi(x)
$$
To first oreder
$$
S^{(1)}=ie\int d^4x T[\bar\psi {A \mkern -9.5 mu /}\psi]\
=ie\int d^4x N[(\bar\psi^++\bar\psi^-)({A \mkern -9.5 mu /}^++{A \mkern -9.5 mu /}^-)(\psi^++\psi^-)]
$$
There are eight terms and all of them are not possible for real physical electron-photon process.？？？？？？

Note $T=N+\text{all possible contractions}$

At second order
$$
S^{(2)}=\sum^m_{i=0} S^{(2)}_i
$$
where $m$ is the maximum number of contractions

$S_0^{(2)}$ is disconnected diagrams; $S^{(2)}1$ has electron-photon scattering and electron-electron scattering;$S{2}^{(2)}$ is the electron self-energy and photon self-energy; $S_3^{(2)}$ is the vaccum bubble.

We use the standard formula for the S-matrix element $\langle f|S| i\rangle$ in terms of the invariant matrix element as
$$
\langle f|S| i\rangle=\delta_{f i}+(2 \pi)^4 \delta^4\left(P_f-P_i\right) \prod_{i=\text { initial particles }} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2 V E_i}} \prod_{f=\text { final particles }} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2 V E_f}} \mathcal{M}{f i}
$$
where $P_f$ and $P_i$ are the sum of 4-momenta of all the particles in final and initial state, respectively, and
$$
\mathcal{M}{f i}=\sum_n \mathcal{M}{f i}^{(n)}
$$
where $M{f i}^{(n)}$ is the invariant matrix element calculated for all the connected diagrams in the $n$th order term in the S-matrix $S^{(n)}$. The calculation of $M_{f i}^{(n)}$ in momentum space is done using the following rules.
