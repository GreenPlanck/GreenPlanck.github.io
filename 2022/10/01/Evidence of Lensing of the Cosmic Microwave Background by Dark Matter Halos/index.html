Evidence of Lensing of the Cosmic Microwave Background by Dark Matter Halos

galaxies selection

  1. The mass of galaxies is estimated through weak-lensing, with the average $\bar M_{200\bar \rho_0}=(2.3\pm 0.1)\times 10^{13}h^{-1}M_{\odot}$
  2. Cut out $70’\times 70’$ stamp; exclude those galaxies whose stamp does not fall entirely within the corresponding ACTPol patch.
  3. What’s the mean of “Inverse variance weight “

pipeline

  1. Set the mean of each stamp to zero

  2. Each stamp is then multiplied by an apodization window = inverse variance weight stamp which is smoothed and tapered with a cosine window

  3. Each stamp is then beam-deconvolved and filtered with quadratic filter ^2 Does this mean to reconstruct kappa field?

    1. On the arcminute scales of individual dark matter halos, the unlensed CMB can be approximated as a gradient, and lensing induced by the halo alters the CMB
      field along this gradient direction. Thus, we search for
      this signal by looking for deflections correlated with the
      background CMB gradient
    2. To realize it, one has to reconstruct $\kappa$ field, using some filters.

  4. Stack with the reconstructed $\kappa$ map

  5. Substract again the “mean field”, which is constructed from ensemble average.

  6. Calculate covariance matrix[^3]

    • Bootstrap: The bootstrapped detection will fluctuate around the real detection, more visualized. It’s more sensitive with samples. If you have a wide range of sample, result may change a lot between two cases which contain more lower samples and another contain more upper.
    • Random shuffle: In simulate case, it can make almost the same result as bootstrap. But in real data, it fails. And it’s more sensitive with background. If there’s a significant background gradient, the result may change a lot between two cases which concentrate on lower side and another higher side.
    • Random offset: It’s almost same with random shuffle if the offset is larger and signals will averaged out. This also often serves as a null test in CMB stacking experiments .
    • Jackknife: It’s artificial to decide how many samples to drop out each time. Too few to underestimater and too many to overestimater.

Construct two versions of filtered data to reconstruct $\kappa$

Filtered1: Isolate the background gradient

The first filtered map is constructed by taking the gradient of the Winer-filtered lensed CMB map
$$
G_{\vec \ell}^{TT}=i\vec\ell W_\ell^{TT}T_{\vec\ell}\\
W_\ell^{TT}=\tilde{C}_\ell^{TT}(C_\ell^{TT}+N_\ell^{TT})^{-1},
$$
where $\tilde{C}_\ell^{TT},C_\ell^{TT}$ are unlensed and lensed CMB theoretical power spectrum respectively.

Filtered2: Isolate small-scale CMB fluctuations

The second map is filtered with inverse-variance filter
$$
L_{\vec \ell}^{TT}=W_\ell^TT_{\vec \ell}\
W_\ell^T=(C_\ell^{TT}+N_\ell^{TT})^{-1}
$$

Take the divergence of the two products gives the estimated $\kappa$

$$
\frac{\kappa_{\vec\ell}^{TT}}{A_\ell^{TT}}=-\int d^2\hat ne^{-i\hat n\cdot\vec\ell}\left[\nabla\cdot\left(G^{TT}(\hat n)L^T(\hat n)\right)\right],\
\kappa^{TT}(\hat n)=\int\frac{d^2\ell}{(2\pi)^2}e^{i\vec\ell\cdot\hat n}\kappa_{\vec\ell}^{TT},\
$$

$$
\frac{1}{A_\ell^{TT}}=\frac{2}{\ell^2}\int\frac{d^2\ell_1}{(2\pi)^2}\vec\ell\cdot\vec\ell_1W_{\ell_1}^{TT}W_{\ell_2}^Tf^{TT}(\vec\ell_1,\vec\ell_2),\
f^{TT}(\vec\ell_1,\vec\ell_2)=\vec\ell\cdot\vec\ell_1\tilde C_{\ell_1}^{TT}+\vec\ell\cdot\vec\ell_2\tilde C_{\ell_2}^{TT},\
\vec\ell=\vec\ell_1+\vec\ell_2
$$

Comment

Those parts about filter in this article read disorderly; In fact the quadrical filter to reconstruct lensing consists of two parts, wiener filter and inverse-variance filter.

SNR from $\chi^2_{null}$

In general $\chi^2$ is calculated as
$$
\chi^2=(measure-model)^TC^{-1}(measure-mode).
$$
A small $\chi^2$ indicates that the measure is consistent with model. If one wants to evaluate the significance of the detection, we take the null hypothesis, setting model=0. And then calculate
$$
\chi^2_{null}=(measure^T) C^{-1}(measure).
$$
If $\chi^2_{null}$ is large, it meas the detection is far from zero, the null detection. And we get the signal to noise.[^3 ]
$$
(\frac{S}{N})^2=\chi^2_{null}
$$

[^3]: Thanks for Denis Tramonte’s guidance

Null tests

In this paper, the author suggests two different null tests, one is random-offset -stacking; another is modify the filter, saying curl test( replace the divergence in filter with curl). The reconstruction is then expected to contain only noise since lensing is not expected to generate a curl signal in temperature maps.
