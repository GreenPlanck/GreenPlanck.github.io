Individual SZ & Observed SZ & Stacked SZ

This note is about the derivation of following equations[^Fang]

SZ power spectrum
$$
\begin{gathered}
C_\ell^{\rm yy,1h}=f(x)^2\int dz\frac{d^2V}{dzd\Omega}\int dM\frac{dn}{dM}(M,z)|\tilde y_\ell(M,z)|^2,\\
C_\ell^{\rm yy,2h}=f(x)^2\int dz\frac{d^2V}{dzd\Omega}P_m^L(k=\frac{\ell}{m},z)W_\ell^y(z)^2,\\
W_\ell^y(z)=\int dM\frac{dn}{dM}(M,z)b(M,z)\tilde y_\ell(M,z)
\end{gathered}
$$

Distribution of galaxy clusters
$$
\begin{gathered}
C_{\ell,\rm{(ab,a’b’)}}^{\rm hh}=
\int dz\frac{d^2V}{dzd\Omega}P_m^L(k=\frac{\ell}{m},z)W_{\rm ab}^{\rm h}(z)W_{\rm a’b’}^{\rm h}(z),\\
W_{\rm ab}^{\rm h}(z)=\frac{1}{\bar n_{\rm ab}^{\rm 2D}}
\int dM\frac{dn}{dM}(M,z)S_{\rm ab}(M,z) b(M,z)
\end{gathered}
$$

Stacked SZ
$$
\begin{gathered}
C_{\ell,\rm(ab)}^{\rm yh}=C_{\ell,\rm(ab)}^{\rm yh,1h}+
C_{\ell,\rm(ab)}^{\rm yh,2h},\\
C_{\ell,\rm(ab)}^{\rm yh,1h}=\frac{f(x)}{\bar n_{\rm ab}^{\rm 2D}}
\int dz\frac{d^2V}{dzd\Omega}\int dM\frac{dn}{dM}(M,z)
S_{\rm ab}(M,z)\tilde y_\ell(M,z),\\
C_{\ell,\rm(ab)}^{\rm yh,2h}=f(x)\int dz\frac{d^2V}{dzd\Omega}P_m^L(k=\frac\ell{m},z)W_{\rm ab}^{\rm h}(z)W_\ell^y(z)\\
\langle y\rangle_{\rm (ab)}(\theta)\equiv \frac{1}{f(x)}\int \frac{\ell d\ell}{2\pi}J_0(\ell\theta)C_{\ell,\rm(ab)}^{\rm yh}
\end{gathered}
$$

Covariance matrix
$$
\begin{gathered}
\rm{Cov}(C_{\ell,\rm(ab)}^{\rm yh},C_{\ell’,\rm(a’b’)}^{\rm yh})
=\frac{\delta_{\ell\ell’}^K}{f_{\rm sky}(2\ell+1)\Delta\ell}
\left[
\hat C_\ell^{\rm yy}\hat C_{\ell,\rm (ab,a’b’)}^{\rm hh}+
\hat C_{\ell,\rm (ab)}^{\rm yh}\hat C_{\ell,\rm (a’b’)}^{\rm yh}
\right],\\
\hat C_\ell^{\rm yy}=C_\ell^{\rm yy}+\frac{1}{\sum_c w_cs_c^2B_{c\ell}^2},\\
\hat C_\ell^{\rm yh}=C_\ell^{\rm yh},\\
\hat C_{\ell,\rm (ab,a’b’)}^{\rm hh}=C_{\ell,\rm (ab,a’b’)}^{\rm hh}+\frac{1}{\bar n_{\rm ab}^{\rm 2D}}\delta_{\rm aa’}^K\delta_{\rm bb’}^K,\\
\rm {Cov}(\langle y\rangle_{\rm (ab)}(\theta_i),\langle y\rangle_{\rm (a’b’)}(\theta_j))
=\frac{1}{\Omega_sf(x)^2}\int\frac{\ell d \ell}{2\pi}\bar J_0(\ell\theta_i)\bar J_0(\ell\theta_j)(\hat C_\ell^{\rm yy}\hat C_{\ell,\rm (ab,a’b’)}^{\rm hh}+
\hat C_{\ell,\rm (ab)}^{\rm yh}\hat C_{\ell,\rm (a’b’)}^{\rm yh})
\end{gathered}
$$

General formalism[^1]

First: angular ps

Assuming source intensity $I_g$ along a given direction $\hat n$ is given as
$$
I_g(\hat n)=\int d\chi g(\chi,\vec r)\tilde W(\chi),\vec r=\chi\hat n
$$
$g(\chi,\vec r)$ is the density field of the source, and it’s a function of the time & space; the tricky is that distance plays the role of redshift at the same time; $\tilde W(\chi)$ is the window function. By define a normalized version of the window function as $W(\chi)=\langle g\rangle\tilde W(\chi)$ , then the average intensity is obtained $\langle I_g\rangle=\int d\chi W(\chi)$
$$
\begin{gathered}
\delta I_g(\hat n)\equiv I_g(\hat n)-\langle I_g\rangle\\
\delta I_g(\hat n)=\langle I_g\rangle\sum_{\ell m}a_{\ell m}Y_{\ell m}(\hat n)\\
a_{\ell m}=\frac{1}{\langle I_g\rangle}\int d\hat n
\delta I_g(\hat n) Y_{\ell m}^*(\hat n)=
\frac{1}{\langle I_g\rangle}\int d\hat n d\chi
(\frac{g}{\delta g}-1)W(\chi) Y_{\ell m}^*(\hat n)=
\frac{1}{\langle I_g\rangle}\int d\hat n d\chi
f_g(\chi,\textcolor{red}{\vec r})W(\chi) Y_{\ell m}^*(\hat n)
\end{gathered}
$$
Inserting the fourier transformation of $f_g(\chi,\vec r)=\int \frac{d^3 k}{(2\pi)^3}\hat f_g(\chi,\vec k)e^{i\vec k\cdot\vec r}$, and spherical expansion of the plane wave $e^{i\vec k \cdot \vec x}=4\pi\sum_\ell i^\ell j_\ell(kx)\sum_m Y_{\ell m}(\hat k)Y_{\ell m}^*(\hat x)$
$$
\begin{gathered}
a_{\ell m}=\frac{1}{\langle I_g\rangle}\int d\hat n d\chi\frac{d^3 k}{2\pi^2}
\hat f_g(\chi,\vec k)W(\chi) Y_{\ell m}^*(\hat n)
\sum_{\ell’}i^{\ell’}j_{\ell’}(k\chi)\sum_{m’}Y_{\ell’m’}(\hat n)Y_{\ell’m’}^*(\hat k)\\
=\frac{i^\ell}{\langle I_g\rangle}\int d\chi\frac{d^3 k}{2\pi^2}
\hat f_g(\chi,\vec k)W(\chi)
j_\ell(k\chi)Y_{\ell m}^*(\hat k)\\
=\frac{i^\ell}{\langle I_g\rangle}\int d\chi W(\chi)
\int\frac{d^3 k}{2\pi^2}
\hat f_g(\chi,\vec k)
j_\ell(k\chi)Y_{\ell m}^*(\hat k)
\end{gathered}
$$

The angular power spectrum between two signals i,j (e.g., i=shear, j=cmb)is given as
$$
\begin{gathered}
C_\ell^{\rm (ij)}=\langle a_{\ell m}^{\rm (i)}
a_{\ell m}^{\rm(j)\star} \rangle \\
=\frac{1}{\langle I_i\rangle\langle I_j\rangle}
\int d\chi W_i(\chi)\int d\chi’ W_j(\chi’)
\int \frac{d^3k}{2\pi^2}\int \frac{d^3k’}{2\pi^2}
\langle \hat f_i(\chi,\vec k)\hat f_j^*(\chi’,\vec k’)\rangle
j_\ell(k\chi)j_{\ell’}(k’\chi’)
Y_{\ell m}(\hat k)Y_{\ell’ m’}^*(\hat k’)\\
=
\frac{2}{\pi}
\frac{1}{\langle I_i\rangle\langle I_j\rangle}
\int d\chi W_i(\chi)\int d\chi’ W_j(\chi’)
\int d^3k
P_{\rm ij}(k,\chi,\chi’)
j_\ell(k\chi)j_{\ell’}(k’\chi’)
Y_{\ell m}(\hat k)Y_{\ell’ m’}^*(\hat k’)\\
\end{gathered}
$$
where we have used $\langle \hat f_i(\chi,\vec k)\hat f_j^*(\chi’,\vec k’)\rangle=(2\pi)^3\delta^3(\vec k -\vec k’)P_{\rm ij}(k,\chi,\chi’)$.

To go further we have to use $\int d\Omega Y_{\ell m}^*(\hat n)Y_{\ell’ m’}(\hat n)=\delta_{\ell\ell’}\delta_{mm’}$ and Limber approximation $\frac{2}{\pi}\int k^2 dk j_\ell(k\chi)j_\ell(k\chi’)=\frac{1}{\chi^2}\delta_D^{(1)}(\chi-\chi’)$ & $P_{ij}(\vec k)$ is independent of $k$
$$
\begin{gathered}
C_\ell^{\rm (ij)}
=
\frac{2}{\pi}
\frac{1}{\langle I_i\rangle\langle I_j\rangle}
\int d\chi W_i(\chi)\int d\chi’ W_j(\chi’)
\int k^2dk
P_{\rm ij}(k,\chi,\chi’)
j_\ell(k\chi)j_{\ell}(k\chi’)\\
=
\frac{1}{\langle I_i\rangle\langle I_j\rangle}
\int \frac{d\chi}{\chi^2}
W_i(\chi) W_j(\chi)P_{\rm ij}(k=\ell/\chi,\chi)
\end{gathered}
$$

The specific 3D $P_{\rm ij}$ : using halo mode

Assuming that the density field can be expressed as the sum of independent seeds :
$$
f(\vec x)=\sum_af(m_a,\vec x-\vec x_a)=\int dm\int d^3x’
\sum_a \delta^3(\vec x’-\vec x_a)\delta(m-m_a)f(m,\vec x-\vec x’)
$$
The seed density can be expressed as $\textcolor{red}{dn/dm=\langle \sum_a \delta^3(\vec x’-\vec x_a)\delta(m-m_a)\rangle}$

The two-point correlation function is
$$
\begin{gathered}
\xi_{\rm ij}(\vec x,\vec y)\equiv
\langle f_i(\vec x) f_j(\vec y)\rangle \\
=\int dm_1dm_2d^3x_1d^3x_2
\langle
\sum_a \delta^3(\vec x_1-\vec x_a)\delta(m_1-m_a)
\sum_b \delta^3(\vec x_2-\vec x_b)\delta(m_2-m_b)
\rangle
f_i(m_1,\vec x-\vec x_1)f_j(m_2,\vec y-\vec x_2)
\end{gathered}
$$

$$
\langle
\sum_a \delta^3(\vec x_1-\vec x_a)\delta(m_1-m_a)
\sum_b \delta^3(\vec x_2-\vec x_b)\delta(m_2-m_b)
\rangle=\frac{dn}{dm_1}\frac{dn}{dm_2}[1+\xi_s(m_1,m_2,\vec x_1,\vec x_2)]+\frac{dn}{dm_1}\delta^3(\vec x_1-\vec x_2)\delta(m_1-m_2)
$$

After inserting it back into 2PCF and fourier transfor, we get the 3D power spectrum,
$$
P_{\rm ij}(k)=\int dm \frac{dn}{dm}\hat f_i^*(k|m)\hat f_j(k|m)+\int dm_1dm_2\frac{dn}{dm_1}\frac{dn}{dm_2}
\hat f_i^*(k|m_1)\hat f_j(k|m_2)P^{\rm (s)}(k,m_1,m_2)
$$
In case of mass density
$$
\begin{gathered}
P^{\rm (s)}(k,m_1,m_2)=b_i(m_1)b_j(m_2)P^{\rm lin}(k)\\
P_{\rm ij}(k)=P_{\rm ij}^{\rm 1h}(k)+P_{\rm ij}^{\rm 2h}(k)\\
P_{\rm ij}^{\rm 1h}(k)=
\int dm \frac{dn}{dm}\hat f_i^*(k|m)\hat f_j(k|m)\\
P_{\rm ij}^{\rm 2h}(k)
=\left[\int dm_1\frac{dn}{dm_1}b_i(m_1)\hat f_i^*(k|m_1)\right]
\left[\int dm_2\frac{dn}{dm_2}b_j(m_2)\hat f_j(k|m_2)\right]
P^{\rm (s)}(k,m_1,m_2)
\end{gathered}
$$

Back to SZ case

For individual effect we use upper character, for LOS effect we use Flower Body

We have
$$
\begin{gathered}
\frac{\Delta T}{T}(x,\hat n)=f(x)\mathcal{Y}(\hat n)\\
f(x)=x\coth(\frac{x}{2})-4\\
\mathcal{Y}(\hat n)=\frac{\sigma_T}{m_ec^2}\int \mathcal{P}_e d\ell
\end{gathered}
$$
We want to express y in terms of source denstiy and window function and replace the line of sight integration with integral along the comoving distance.
$$
d\ell=cdt=ad\chi\propto \chi^2 d\chi
$$

where we have used that at matter domain $a\propto \chi^2$

$$
\mathcal{Y}(\hat n)=\frac{\sigma_T}{m_ec^2}\int a_c\chi^2\mathcal{P}e(\chi,\hat n)d\chi\equiv \int \mathcal{S}(\chi,\hat n)\chi^2d\chi
$$
Then we have that $W(\chi)=\chi^2,g(\chi,\hat n)=S(\chi,\hat n)$
$$
\begin{gathered}
\mathcal{Y}\rightarrow a{\ell m}=\int d\hat n d\chi\frac{d^3 k}{2\pi^2}
\mathcal{S}(\chi,\vec k)W(\chi) Y_{\ell m}^*(\hat n)
\sum_{\ell’}i^{\ell’}j_{\ell’}(k\chi)\sum_{m’}Y_{\ell’m’}(\hat n)Y_{\ell’m’}^*(\hat k)\\
=i^\ell\int d\chi\frac{d^3 k}{2\pi^2}
\mathcal{S}(\chi,\vec k)W(\chi)
j_\ell(k\chi)Y_{\ell m}^*(\hat k)\\
=i^\ell\int d\chi W(\chi)
\int\frac{d^3 k}{2\pi^2}
\mathcal{S}(\chi,\vec k)
j_\ell(k\chi)Y_{\ell m}^*(\hat k)
\end{gathered}
$$

$$
\begin{gathered}
C_\ell^{\rm (ij)}=\langle a_{\ell m}
a_{\ell m}^{\star} \rangle \\
=
\int d\chi W_i(\chi)\int d\chi’ W_j(\chi’)
\int \frac{d^3k}{2\pi^2}\int \frac{d^3k’}{2\pi^2}
\langle \mathcal{S}(\chi,\vec k)\mathcal{S}^*(\chi’,\vec k’)\rangle
j_\ell(k\chi)j_{\ell’}(k’\chi’)
Y_{\ell m}(\hat k)Y_{\ell’ m’}^*(\hat k’)\\
=
\frac{2}{\pi}
\int d\chi W_i(\chi)\int d\chi’ W_j(\chi’)
\int d^3k
P_{\rm ij}(k,\chi,\chi’)
j_\ell(k\chi)j_{\ell’}(k’\chi’)
Y_{\ell m}(\hat k)Y_{\ell’ m’}^*(\hat k’)\\
\end{gathered}
$$

$$
\mathcal{S}(\vec x)=\sum_af(m_a,\vec x-\vec x_a)=\int dm\int d^3x’
\sum_a \delta^3(\vec x’-\vec x_a)\delta(m-m_a)S(m,\vec x-\vec x’)
$$

$$
\begin{gathered}
\xi(\vec x,\vec y)=\xi^{\rm 1h}(\vec x,\vec y)+\xi^{\rm 2h}(\vec x,\vec y)\\
\xi^{\rm 1h}(\vec x,\vec y)=\int dm d^3x_1\frac{dn}{dm}S(\vec x-\vec x_1,m)S(\vec y-\vec x_1,m)\\
\xi^{\rm 2h}(\vec x,\vec y)=\int dm_1dm_2d^3x_1d^3x_2\frac{dn}{dm_1}\frac{dn}{dm_2}S(\vec x-\vec x_1,m_1)S(\vec y-\vec x_2,m_2)\xi_s(m_1,m_2,\vec x_1,\vec x_2)
\end{gathered}
$$
For simplicity, we take S(\chi,\vec r)$ as our normlised overdensity $f_g$; all the constants are dropped for temperary.

We can then easily get the 3D power spectrum for Compton y by inserting our new $f_g$ into previous results
$$
\begin{gathered}
P^{\rm yy,1h}(k)=\int dm \frac{dn}{dm}|S(k|m)|^2,\\
P^{\rm yy,2h}(k)=\left[\int dm \frac{dn}{dm}b(m)S(k|m)\right]^2P^{\rm lin}(k)
\end{gathered}
$$
What is tricky is that we want to rewrite the 3D fourier transformation of the preesure into 2D fourier transformation of the Compton y of an individual cluster.
$$
y(\hat n)=\int d\chi \chi^2S(\chi,\vec r)\\
y(\vec \ell)=\int d\Omega e^{-i\vec \ell \cdot \hat n}\int d\chi\chi^2 S(\chi,\vec r)=\int dV e^{-i\vec k \vec r}P_e(\chi,\vec r)e^{ik\chi}=e^{i\ell}S(\chi,\vec k)
$$
where we have used that $a\propto \chi^2$ at matter domain thus $\chi^2d\chi=ad\chi=cdt=d\ell$; we also define the relation between the vectors as $\vec k=(k,\vec \ell),\vec r=(\chi,\hat n)$ and the Limber approximation $\ell=k\chi$.

Since $P_e(\chi,\vec k)$ always appears in couples, then $e^{i\ell}(e^{i\ell})^*=1$, we can replace it with the two dimension transofrmation of Compton y.

Lastly, the structure of $C_\ell$ we derived above is $C_\ell=\int\frac{d\chi}{\chi^2}W^2P(k)$. As we have showed, in matter domain, the window function for y is $\chi^2$ ,we can then write $\chi^2d\chi=\frac{c\chi^2}{H}dz=dz\frac{d^2V}{dzd\Omega}$
$$
\begin{gathered}
C_\ell^{\rm yy,1h}=f(x)^2\int dz\frac{d^2V}{dzd\Omega}\int dM\frac{dn}{dM}(M,z)|\tilde y_\ell(M,z)|^2,\\
C_\ell^{\rm yy,2h}=f(x)^2\int dz\frac{d^2V}{dzd\Omega}P_m^L(k=\frac{\ell}{m},z)W_\ell^y(z)^2,\\
W_\ell^y(z)=\int dM\frac{dn}{dM}(M,z)b(M,z)\tilde y_\ell(M,z)
\end{gathered}
$$

Galaxy clusters’ distribution

The angular number density of galaxy clusters in the (ab)-th bin can be calculated by
$$
\begin{aligned}
n_{a b}^{2 D}(\hat{n})=& \int d z \frac{d^{2} V}{d z d \Omega} \int d M S_{a b}(M, z) \
& \times \sum_{i} \delta_{D}\left(M-M_{i}\right) \delta_{D}^{3}\left(\vec{x}-\vec{x}_{i}\right),
\end{aligned}
$$

The stacked SZ profile

$$
\begin{aligned}
&C_{\ell,(a b)}^{y \mathrm{h}, \mathrm{lh}}=\frac{f(x)}{\bar{n}{a b}^{2 D}} \int d z \frac{d^{2} V}{d z d \Omega} \int d M \frac{d n}{d M}\
&\times(M, z) S{a b}(M, z) \tilde{y}{\ell}(M, z),\
&C{\ell,(a b)}^{y \mathrm{h}, 2 \mathrm{~h}}=f(x) \int d z \frac{d^{2} V}{d z d \Omega} P_{m}^{L}\left(k=\frac{\ell}{\chi}, z\right) W_{a b}^{h}(z) W_{\ell}^{y}(z)
\end{aligned}
$$

$$
\langle y\rangle_{(a b)}(\theta) \equiv \frac{1}{f(x)} \int \frac{\ell d \ell}{2 \pi} J_{0}(\ell \theta) C_{\ell,(a b)}^{y \mathrm{~h}}
$$
