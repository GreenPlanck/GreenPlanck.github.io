Halo mass function

$\sigma(M)\equiv\sigma(R_{\rm L}(M),z)$

Equation 12.73
$$
\begin{gathered}
\frac{dn(M,z)}{d\ln M}=\frac{\rho_m(t_0)}{M}f_{\rm PS}(\frac{\delta_{\rm cr}}{\sigma(M,z)})\left|\frac{d\ln \sigma(M,z)}{d\ln M}\right|\\
f_{\rm PS}(\nu)=\sqrt\frac{2}{\pi}\nu e^{-\nu^2/2}
\end{gathered}
$$
Note that, in the literature, our $f_{\rm PS}(\nu)$ is also frequently defined with a factor $\nu$ outside, i.e. $f_{\rm PS}(\nu) \rightarrow \nu f_{\rm PS}(\nu)$

“Let $n(m,z)$ denote the comoving number density of bound objects, halos, of
mass m at redshift z. (Some authors use $dn/dm$ to denote this same quantity,
and we will use the two notations interchangeably.) “

Equation56
$$
n=f(\nu)\frac{\bar\rho}{m}\frac{d\nu}{dm}\\
\nu f(\nu)=\sqrt{\frac{\nu}{2\pi}}\exp{(-\nu/2)}\\
\nu\equiv\frac{\delta_{\rm sc}^2(z)}{\sigma^2}
$$

we can combine this two definition by calculating
$$
\begin{gathered}
\nu\equiv\frac{\sigma_{\rm sc}^2(z)}{\sigma^2}\rightarrow
d\nu=-2\frac{\delta_{\rm sc}(z)^2}{\sigma^2}\frac{1}{\sigma}d\sigma=-2\nu d\ln\sigma\\
n=f(\nu)\frac{\bar \rho}{m}(-2\nu)\frac{d\ln \sigma}{dm}\\
=-\sqrt\frac{2}{\pi}
\sqrt\nu\exp{(-\nu/2)}\frac{\bar\rho}{m}\frac{d\ln \sigma}{dm}
\end{gathered}
$$
which is consistent with the one in Moder Cosmology.

$\sigma^2(m)$ is the variance in the initial density fluctuation field when smoothed with a tophat filter of scale $R = (3m/4\pi \bar\rho)^{1/3}$, extrapolated to the present time using linear theory
$$
\sigma^2_{\rm lim}(m)\equiv\int\frac{dk}{k}\frac{k^3P^{\rm lin}(k)}{2\pi^2}\left|W(kR)\right|^2,W(x)=\frac{3}{x^3}\left(\sin(x)-x\cos(x)\right)
$$

Equation59 gives Sheth&Tormen:
$$
\begin{gathered}
\nu f(\nu)=A(p)(1+(q\nu)^{-p})(\frac{q\nu}{2\pi})^{1/2}\exp{(-q\nu/2)}\\
p\simeq 0.3\\
A(p)=\left[
1+2^{-p}\Gamma(1/2-p)/\sqrt{\pi}
\right]^{-1}\simeq 0.3222\\
q\simeq 0.75
\end{gathered}
$$
If p=1/2,q=1, then ST is the same as PS.

We can rewrite the previous as the definition of $f(\nu)$
$$
f(\sigma,z)\equiv \frac{m}{\bar \rho}\frac{dn}{d\ln (\sigma^{-1})}
$$

Jenkins 2001

“It proves convenient to use the quantity $\ln \sigma^{-1}(M, z)$ as the mass variable instead of $M$, where $\sigma^2(M,z)$ is the variance of the linear density field, extrapolated to the redshift z at which haloes are identified, after smoothing with a spherical top-hat filter which encloses mass M in the mean “
$$
\sigma^2(M,z)=\frac{b^2(z)}{2\pi^2}\int^\infty_0k^2P(k)W^2(k;M)dk
$$
$b(z)$ is the growth factor of linear perturbations normalized so that b=1 at z=0.

Define mass function through
$$
f(\sigma,z;M)\equiv\frac{M}{\rho_0}\frac{dn(M,z)}{d\ln \sigma^{-1}}
$$

$$
f(\sigma,\rm PS)=\sqrt\frac{2}{\pi}\frac{\delta_{\rm sc}}{\sigma}\exp{(-\frac{\delta_{\rm sc}^2}{2\sigma^2})}
$$

$$
\begin{gathered}
f(\sigma,\rm ST)=A\sqrt\frac{2a}{\pi}\left[1+(\frac{\sigma^2}{a\delta_{\rm sc}^2})^p
\right]
\frac{\delta_{\rm sc}}{\sigma}\exp{(-\frac{a\delta_{\rm sc}^2}{2\sigma^2})}\\
A=0.3222,a=0.707,p=0.3
\end{gathered}
$$

Fitting model

B3:
$$
f(M)=0.301\exp{(-\left|\ln \sigma^{-1}+0.64 \right|^{3.82})}
$$

Tinker2008^cluster_toolkit

Eq2
$$
\begin{gathered}
\frac{dn}{dM}=f(\sigma)\frac{\bar \rho_m}{M}\frac{\ln \sigma^{-1}}{dM}\\
f(\sigma)=A\left[1+(\frac{\sigma}{b})^{-a}\right]e^{-c/\sigma^2}\\
\sigma=\int P(k)\hat W(kR)k^2 dk\\
R=(3M/4\pi\bar\rho_m)^{1/3}
\end{gathered}
$$
An alternative (more often refered) C2
$$
g(\sigma)=B\left[\sigma^{-f}+(\frac{\sigma}{e})^{-d}\right]e^{-g/\sigma^2}
$$
The values of those parameters can be found in the table of that paper.
