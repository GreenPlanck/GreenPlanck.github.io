Power spectrum & Transfer function & Source function

This note is about the following equaitons.
$$
<h^\lambda(k_1)h^{\lambda’}(k_2)>=\frac{(2\pi)^3}{2}\delta(k_1+k_2)P_h(k)\delta^{\lambda\lambda’}
$$

$$
P_h(k)=rA_sk^{-3}(\frac{k}{k_0})^{n_T}
$$

$$
B_{\ell m}=4\pi(-i)^\ell\int\frac{d^3k} { (2\pi)^3}\sum_{\pm} { _ {\pm 2}Y^*_{\ell m} }(\hat k)h^{\pm}(k)T_{\ell}^B(k)
$$

$$
T_\ell^B(k)=\sqrt{\frac{\pi^2}{8}}\int_0^{\eta_0}d\eta S_P^{(h)}(k,\eta)\hat{B}(k\eta)\frac{j_\ell(k\eta)}{(k\eta)^2}
$$

$$
\hat{B}(x)=8x+2x^2\partial_x^2,S_P^{(h)}(k,\eta)=-g(\eta)\Psi(k,\eta)
$$

where T is transfer function and S is source function[^1]

[^1]: https://arxiv.org/abs/1610.09365

A first glance

Starting from galaxy overdensity
$$
\delta(\hat r)=\frac{1}{V_s}\int^\infty_0\delta(\vec{r})S(r)r^2dr
$$
where $$ S(r) $$ plays the role of selection function and also serves as the source function in other cases.

Fourier transform and write plane wave in spherical harmonicas
$$
\delta(\vec r)=\sum_{\vec k}\delta_{\vec k}e^{i\vec k\cdot \vec r}=4\pi\sum_{\vec k,\ell,m}\delta_{\vec k}i^\ell j_{\ell}(kr)Y_{\ell m}(\hat r)Y_{\ell m}^*(\hat k).
$$
Recall that $$a_{\ell m}=\int d\Omega Y_{\ell m}^*(\hat r)\delta(\hat r)$$
$$
\begin{gathered}
a_{\ell m}=\int d\Omega Y_{\ell m}^*(\hat r)[\frac{1}{V_s}\int_0^\infty \delta(\vec r)S(r)r^2dr]\\
=\int d\Omega Y_{\ell m}^*(\hat r)[\frac{1}{V_s}\int_0^\infty(4\pi\sum_{\vec k,\ell,m}\delta_{\vec k}j_{\ell}(kr)Y_{\ell m}(\hat r)Y_{\ell m}^*(\hat k))S(r)r^2dr]\\
=\frac{4\pi i^\ell }{V_s}\sum_{\vec k}\delta_{\vec k}\int_0^\infty j_{\ell}(kr)Y_{\ell m}^*(\hat k)S(r)r^2dr
\end{gathered}
$$
where we have used $$ \int d\Omega Y_{\ell m}^*(\hat r)Y_{\ell’ m’}(\hat r)=\delta_{\ell \ell’}\delta_{m m’}$$.

Replace the sum $$\frac{1}{V_s}\sum_{\vec k}$$ with the integral $$\frac{1}{(2\pi)^3}\int d^3k$$ , we then have
$$
a_{\ell m}=4\pi i^\ell\int\frac{d^3k}{(2\pi)^3} \delta_{\vec k}Y_{\ell m}^*(\hat k)\int_0^\infty j_\ell(kr)S(r)r^2dr
\label{9}
$$
Eq9 is just the same form of Eq 3 if we define $$T_\ell\equiv \int_0^\infty j_\ell(kr)S(r)r^2dr$$

In other papers, another notation is usually adopted, as $$\Delta_\ell^X\equiv \int_0^\infty j_\ell(kr)S(r)r^2dr$$.

Angular power spectrum is then
$$
C_\ell=\frac{1}{2\ell+1}\sum_m<|a_{\ell m}|^2>
=\frac{2}{\pi}\int dk k^2P(k)\Delta_\ell(k)^2
$$
$$\textcolor{purple}{The;coefficient;2\pi; may ;change ;between ;different ;conventions;}$$

[^2]:Chapter 5 https://www.mv.helsinki.fi/home/hkurkisu/GSC1.pdf

CMB scalar temperature power spectrum

What’s the source function in CMB case? This can be obtained by line of sight integration of the Boltzmann equation.

The temperature anisotropy is denoted with $$\Delta_T^{(S)}(\tau,k,\mu)$$ and in the synchronous gauge the Boltzann equation is written as
$$
\begin{gathered}
\dot\Delta_T^{ (S)}+ik\mu\Delta_T^{ (S)}=-\frac{1}{6}\dot h-\frac{1}{6}(\dot h+6\dot \eta)P_2(\mu)+\dot\kappa[-\Delta_T^{ (S)}+\Delta_{T0}^{ (S)}+i\mu v_b+\frac{1}{2}P_2(\mu)\Pi],\\
\Pi=\Delta_{T2}^{ (S)}+\Delta_{P2}^{ (S)}+\Delta_{P0}^{ (S)}
\end{gathered}
$$
The LOS solution is
$$
\begin{gathered}
\Delta_T^{(S)}(\tau_0,k,\mu)=\int_0^{\tau_0}d\tau e^{ix\mu}S_T^{(S)}(k,\tau),\\
S_T^{(S)}(k,\tau)=g(\Delta_{T,0}+2\dot\alpha+\frac{\dot v_b}{k}+\frac{\Pi}{4}+\frac{3\ddot\Pi}{4k^2})+e^{-\kappa}(\dot\eta+\ddot\alpha)+\dot g(\alpha+\frac{v_b}{k}+\frac{3\dot\Pi}{4k^2})+\frac{3\ddot g\Pi}{4k^2}
\end{gathered}
$$

$$
C_{T\ell}^{(s)}=(4\pi)^2\int k^2dk P_{\phi}(k)\left[\int_0^{\tau_0} S_T^{(S)}(k,\tau)j_\ell(x)\right]^2
$$

The LOS solution just implies that $$\Delta_{T\ell}^{(s)}=\int_0^{\tau_0} S_T^{(S)}(k,\tau)j_\ell(x)$$;
$$
<\xi^*(\vec{k}_1)\xi(\vec{k}_2)>=P_\phi(k)\delta(\vec{k}_1-\vec{k}_2).
$$

CMB tensor B mode power spectrum

Firstly, introduce a variable transformation
$$
\begin{gathered}
(\Delta_Q^{(T)}+i\Delta_U^{(T)})(\tau_0,\hat n,\vec k)=[(1-\mu)^2 e^{2i\phi}\xi^1(\vec k)+(1+\mu)^2e^{-2i\phi}\xi^2(\vec k)]\tilde{\Delta}_P^{(T)}(\tau,\mu,k),\\
(\Delta_Q^{(T)}-i\Delta_U^{(T)})(\tau_0,\hat n,\vec k)=[(1+\mu)^2 e^{2i\phi}\xi^1(\vec k)+(1-\mu)^2e^{-2i\phi}\xi^2(\vec k)]\tilde{\Delta}_P^{(T)}(\tau,\mu,k)
\end{gathered}
$$

$$
\begin{gathered}
\xi^1=(\xi^+-i\xi^\times)/\sqrt{2},\\
\xi^2=(\xi^++i\xi^\times)/\sqrt{2},\\
S_P^{(T)}(k,\tau)=-g\Psi
\end{gathered}
$$

The variable $$\tilde\Delta_P^{(T)}$$ then follows a simple boltzmann equation. Again use LOS integration, we can get the solution
$$
\begin{gathered}
(\Delta_Q^{(T)}+i\Delta_U^{(T)})(\tau_0,\hat n,\vec k)=[(1-\mu)^2 e^{2i\phi}\xi^1(\vec k)+(1+\mu)^2e^{-2i\phi}\xi^2(\vec k)]\int_0^{\tau_0}d\tau e^{ix\mu}S_P^{(T)}(k,\tau),\\
(\Delta_Q^{(T)}-i\Delta_U^{(T)})(\tau_0,\hat n,\vec k)=[(1+\mu)^2 e^{2i\phi}\xi^1(\vec k)+(1-\mu)^2e^{-2i\phi}\xi^2(\vec k)]\int_0^{\tau_0}d\tau e^{ix\mu}S_P^{(T)}(k,\tau)
\end{gathered}
$$
To continue, we should act twice raising or lowering operator on Eq17 to get the B mode transfer function,
$$
\begin{gathered}
\tilde B(\hat n)\equiv \frac{i}{2}[\bar\partial^2(Q+iU)-\partial^2(Q-iU)],\\
\Delta_{\tilde B}^{(T)}(\tau_0,\hat n,\vec k)=
[(1-\mu^2) e^{2i\phi}\xi^1(\vec k)-(1-\mu^2)e^{-2i\phi}\xi^2(\vec k)]\hat{\mathcal B}(x)\int_0^{\tau_0}d\tau e^{ix\mu}S_P^{(T)}(k,\tau)
\end{gathered}
$$
