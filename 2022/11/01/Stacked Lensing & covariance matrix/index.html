Stacked Lensing & covariance matrix

Let us assume that we have a sample of haloes distributed over a solid angle of the survey field. Then consider a case that we use this sample of haloes for the stacked lensing measurement by cross-correlating positions of haloes with shapes of background galaxies.

Preliminaries

  • Halo & Lensing

$$
\begin{gathered}
\bar n {\rm h}^{\rm 2d}=\int {\chi_l-\Delta\chi_l/2}^{\chi_l-\Delta\chi+l/2}
d\chi \chi^2f{\rm h}(\chi)\int dM\frac{dn}{dM}S(M,\chi)\
\delta{\rm h}^{\rm 2d}(\vec\theta)\equiv
\frac{1}{\bar n_{\rm h}}
\int {\chi_l-\Delta\chi_l/2}^{\chi_l-\Delta\chi+l/2}
d\chi \chi^2f{\rm h}(\chi)\int dM\frac{dn}{dM}S(M,\chi)\delta_{\rm h}(\chi\vec\theta,\chi;M)
\end{gathered}
$$

Here $$\chi_l$$ is the mean radial distance to halos in the sample and $\Delta\chi_l$ is the width of there radial distances.

  • Source

Suppose that the redshift distribution of source galaxies is given by
$$
\frac{dn}{dz_s}dz_s=\bar n _{\rm tot}p(z_s)dz_s
$$
where $$\bar n$$ is the angular number density, different from halo’s 3d number density $dn/dM$, and $p(z_s)$ is the normalized redshift distribution, defined so as to $\int_0^\infty dz_sp(z_s)=1$.
$$
p(z_s)\propto z_s^2\exp\left[-\frac{z_s}{z_0}\right]
$$

The cosmic shear effect(also kensing covergence) on a source galaxy in the angular direction $\theta_s$ and at redshift zs is caused by foreground structures along the path of light ray
$$
\kappa(\vec\theta_s,z_s)\equiv\int^{z_s}0d\chi \Sigma{\rm cr}^{-1}(z,z_s)\bar\rho_{m0}\delta_m(\chi,\chi\vec\theta_s)
$$
Stack means
$$
<\Sigma>(R)\equiv\frac{1}{N_{w,pair}(R)}\sum_{l,s} w_{ls}\kappa(\vec\theta_s,z_s)|{R=\chi_l|\vec\theta_l-\vec\theta_s|}
$$
we will take the weight w equal to one for easy; $N{w,pair}$ is the weighted number of pairs used in the summation of each radial bin. We take equal weight $w=1$,then
$$
\begin{gathered}
<\Sigma>(R)=\frac{1}{N}\int_{z_{\rm cut}}^\infty dz_sp(z_s)<\delta^{\rm 2d}h(\chi,\chi\vec\theta_l)\kappa(\vec\theta_s,z_s)>|R\
=\frac{1}{N}\int{z{\rm cut}}^\infty dz_sp(z_s)
\frac{1}{\bar n_{\rm h}}
\int {\chi_l-\Delta\chi_l/2}^{\chi_l-\Delta\chi+l/2}
d\chi \chi^2f{\rm h}(\chi)\int dM\frac{dn}{dM}S(M,\chi)
\int^{z_s}0d\chi’ \Sigma{\rm cr}^{-1}(z’,z_s)\bar\rho_{m0}
<\delta_{\rm h}(\chi,\chi\vec\theta_l)\delta_m(\chi’,\chi’\vec\theta_s)>|_R\
R=\chi|\vec\theta_l-\vec\theta_s|
\end{gathered}
$$

We should note that in the first line, we integrate from $z_{cut}$ to infinity, meaning that only those $z>z_{cut}$ could serve as a source
$$
\chi(z_{\rm cut})>\chi_l+\Delta\chi_l/2
$$
It’s still strange compared WMAP paper, where the stack is noted as
$$
(\vec\theta)=\frac{1}{f_{\rm sky}}\int\frac{d^2\hat n}{4\pi}W(\hat n)<\delta_{\rm pk}(\hat n)Q_r(\hat n+\hat \theta)>
$$
One sum over the whole sky and another sum over the whole redshift extension.
