Bispectrum

General

The CMB angular bispectrum is given by
$$
B_{l_{1} l_{2} l_{3}}^{m_{1} m_{2} m_{3}} \equiv\left\langle a_{l_{1} m_{1}} a_{l_{2} m_{2}} a_{l_{3} m_{3}}\right\rangle
$$
The bispectrum $B_{l_{1} l_{2} l_{3}}^{m_{1} m_{2} m_{3}}$ must satisfy the triangle conditions and selection rules : $m_1+m_2+m_3=0,l_1+l_2+l_3={\rm even}, |l_i-l_j|\le l_k\le l_i+l_j$.

Thus, $B_{l_{1} l_{2} l_{3}}^{m_{1} m_{2} m_{3}}$ consists of the Gaunt integral
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathcal{G}{l{1} l_{2} l_{3}}^{m_{1} m_{2} m_{3}} \equiv & \int d^{2} \hat{\mathbf{n}} Y_{l_{1} m_{1}}(\hat{\mathbf{n}}) Y_{l_{2} m_{2}}(\hat{\mathbf{n}}) Y_{l_{3} m_{3}}(\hat{\mathbf{n}}) \
=& \sqrt{\frac{\left(2 l_{1}+1\right)\left(2 l_{2}+1\right)\left(2 l_{3}+1\right)}{4\pi} }\left(\begin{array}{ccc}
l_{1} & l_{2} & l_{3} \
0 & 0 & 0
\end{array}\right) \
& \times\left(\begin{array}{ccc}
l_{1} & l_{2} & l_{3} \
m_{1} & m_{2} & m_{3}
\end{array}\right)
\end{aligned}
$$

$$
B_{l_{1} l_{2} l_{3}}^{m_{1} m_{2} m_{3}}=\mathcal{G}{l{1} l_{2} l_{3}}^{m_{1} m_{2} m_{3}}
b_{l_1l_2l_3}
$$

where $b_{l_1l_2l_3}$ is an arbitrary real symmetric function , called reduced bispectrum. This form is necessary and sufficient to construct generic $B_{l_{1} l_{2} l_{3}}^{m_{1} m_{2} m_{3}}$ under rotational invariance.

The observable quantity, the angle-averaged bistrum is defined as
$$
\begin{aligned}
B_{l_{1} l_{2} l_{3}} \equiv& \sum_{m_{1} m_{2} m_{3}}
\left(\begin{array}{ccc}
l_{1} & l_{2} & l_{3} \
m_{1} & m_{2} & m_{3}
\end{array}\right)
B_{l_{1} l_{2} l_{3}}^{m_{1} m_{2} m_{3}}\

=& \sqrt{\frac{\left(2 l_{1}+1\right)\left(2 l_{2}+1\right)\left(2 l_{3}+1\right)}{4\pi} }\left(\begin{array}{ccc}
l_{1} & l_{2} & l_{3} \
0 & 0 & 0
\end{array}\right)b_{l_1l_2l_3} \
& \times \sum_{m_1m_2m_3}
\left(\begin{array}{ccc}
l_{1} & l_{2} & l_{3} \
m_{1} & m_{2} & m_{3}
\end{array}\right)
\left(\begin{array}{ccc}
l_{1} & l_{2} & l_{3} \
m_{1} & m_{2} & m_{3}
\end{array}\right)\
=&
\sqrt{\frac{\left(2 l_{1}+1\right)\left(2 l_{2}+1\right)\left(2 l_{3}+1\right)}{4\pi} }\left(\begin{array}{ccc}
l_{1} & l_{2} & l_{3} \
0 & 0 & 0
\end{array}\right)b_{l_1l_2l_3}
\end{aligned}
$$
where we have used the relationship in https://functions.wolfram.com/HypergeometricFunctions/ThreeJSymbol/23/ShowAll.html
$$
\sum_{m_2m_3}
\left(\begin{array}{ccc}
l_{1} & l_{2} & l_{3} \
m_{1} & m_{2} & m_{3}
\end{array}\right)
\left(\begin{array}{ccc}
l_{4} & l_{2} & l_{3} \
m_{4} & m_{2} & m_{3}
\end{array}\right)
=\frac{\delta_{l_1l_4}\delta_{m_1m_4}}{2l_1+1}
$$
In flat sky limit, $\mathcal{G}{l_1l_2l_3}^{m_1m_2m_3}\rightarrow \delta^{(2)}(\vec l_1+\vec l_2+\vec l_3)$
$$
\begin{gathered}
<a(\vec l_1)a(\vec l_2)a(\vec l_3)>=(2\pi)^2\delta^{(2)}(\vec l_1+\vec l_2+\vec l_3)B(\vec l_1,\vec l_2,\vec l_3)\\
b{l_1l_2l_3}\simeq B(\vec l_1,\vec l_2,\vec l_3)
\end{gathered}
$$

Primary

The Relationship between primordial adiabatic scalar fluctuations, , and temperature fluctuation’s spherical harmonic coefficient is
$$
a_{l m}=4 \pi(-i)^{l} \int \frac{d^{3} \mathbf{k}}{(2 \pi)^{3}} \Phi(\mathbf{k}) g_{T l}(k) Y_{l m}^{*}(\hat{\mathbf{k}})\
g_{Tl}(k)=\int r^2dr S(r)j_l(kr)
$$
where $\Phi(k)$ is the primordial curvature perturbation, $g_{Tl}$ is the radiation transfer function.

We model $\Phi$ as
$$
\Phi(\vec x)=\Phi_L(\vec x)+f_{\rm NL}\left[
\Phi^2_L(\vec x)-<\Phi^2_L(x)>
\right]
$$
to include non-linear coupling. $\Phi _L$ is the linear Gaussian part and $<\Phi(\vec x)>=0$ is guaranteed.

The first non-zero $\Phi$ field bispectrumis
$$
\begin{aligned}
&\left\langle\Phi\left(\mathbf{k}{1}\right) \Phi\left(\mathbf{k}{2}\right) \Phi\left(\mathbf{k}_{3}\right)\right\rangle \simeq

\left\langle\Phi_{L}\left(\mathbf{k}{1}\right) \Phi{L}\left(\mathbf{k}{2}\right) \Phi{N L}\left(\mathbf{k}{3}\right)\right\rangle
+\left\langle\Phi{L}\left(\mathbf{k}{1}\right) \Phi{NL}\left(\mathbf{k}{2}\right) \Phi{ L}\left(\mathbf{k}{3}\right)\right\rangle
+\left\langle\Phi{NL}\left(\mathbf{k}{1}\right) \Phi{L}\left(\mathbf{k}{2}\right) \Phi{ L}\left(\mathbf{k}_{3}\right)\right\rangle
\

&
\textcolor{red}{
\left\langle\Phi_{L}\left(\mathbf{k}{1}\right) \Phi{L}\left(\mathbf{k}{2}\right) \Phi{N L}\left(\mathbf{k}{3}\right)\right\rangle=2(2 \pi)^{3} \delta^{(3)}\left(\mathbf{k}{1}+\mathbf{k}{2}+\mathbf{k}{3}\right) f_{N L} P_{\Phi}\left(k_{1}\right) P_{\Phi}\left(k_{2}\right)}
\end{aligned}
$$

The CMB angular bispectrum then consists three parts and the first part is
$$
\begin{aligned}
B_{l_1l_2l_3}^{m_1m_2m_3}&=\left(\frac{2}{\pi}(-i)^l\right)^3\int k_1^2dk_1\int k_2^2dk_2 \int k_3^2dk_3
f_{NL}g_{Tl}(k_1)g_{Tl}(k_2)g_{Tl}(k_3)P_\Phi(k_1)P_\Phi(k_2)\
&\times \int d^2\hat k_1\int d^2\hat k_2\int d^2\hat k_3 \delta^{(3)}(\vec k_1+\vec k_2+\vec k_3)Y_{l_1m_1}^*(\hat k_1)Y_{l_2m_2}^*(\hat k_2)Y_{l_3m_3}^*(\hat k_3)

\end{aligned}
$$
whether
$$
\textcolor{red}{
\int d^2\hat k_1\int d^2\hat k_2\int d^2\hat k_3 \delta^{(3)}(\vec k_1+\vec k_2+\vec k_3)Y_{l_1m_1}^*(\hat k_1)Y_{l_2m_2}^*(\hat k_2)Y_{l_3m_3}^*(\hat k_3)\xlongequal[]{?}\mathcal{G_{l_1l_2l_3}^{m_1m_2m_3}}(-i)^{-l}

}
$$
If so, the CMB bispectrum is written as
$$
\begin{aligned}
B_{l_{1} l_{2} l_{3}}^{m_{1} m_{2} m_{3}}=&\left\langle a_{l_{1} m_{1}}^{L} a_{l_{2} m_{2}}^{L} a_{l_{3} m_{3}}^{N L}\right\rangle+\left\langle a_{l_{1} m_{1}}^{L} a_{l_{2} m_{2}}^{N L} a_{l_{3} m_{3}}^{L}\right\rangle \
&+\left\langle a_{l_{1} m_{1}}^{N L} a_{l_{2} m_{2}}^{L} a_{l_{3} m_{3}}^{L}\right\rangle \
=& 2 \mathcal{G}{l{1} l_{2} l_{3}}^{m_{1} m_{2} m_{3}} \int_{0}^{\infty} r^{2} d r\left[b_{l_{1}}^{L}(r) b_{l_{2}}^{L}(r) b_{l_{3}}^{N L}(r)\right.\
&\left.+b_{l_{1}}^{L}(r) b_{l_{2}}^{N L}(r) b_{l_{3}}^{L}(r)+b_{l_{1}}^{N L}(r) b_{l_{2}}^{L}(r) b_{l_{3}}^{L}(r)\right],\
b_l^L(r)=&\frac{2}{\pi}\int k^2dkP_\Phi(k)S(r)j_l(kr),\
b_l^{NL}(r)=&\frac{2}{\pi}\int k^2dk f_{NL}S(r)j_l(kr).
\end{aligned}
$$
