Structure formation with generalized dark matter

Today I want to write a note on the first paper of generalized dark matter model given by Wayne Hu 1998. And I hope to cultivate the habit of note-writing. A short term target is to write a note for the following whole week 2022.12.5.

Besides serving as a material for note-writing, these notes may help the future paper writing.

I’d like to do something like “repeatation” following what Wayne Hu has written but in a manner of myself understanding.

Introduction

With the upcoming more precise cosmic microwave background (CMB) data and larger sclae structure (LSS) data, the unknown dark sector, which is introduced with the simplest model to account for the current observations, will probably be revealed. The cold dark matter (CDM) model may fail then. How might one generalize it and remain the gravitational instability that is homogeneous and isotropic on the large scale? The well-motivated dark matter candidates are weakly interacting massive particles, massive neutrinos, scalar fields and topological defects. What will happen if none of these models survive the modern cosmological data? Can one determine the nature of the dark matter beyond model dependence?
