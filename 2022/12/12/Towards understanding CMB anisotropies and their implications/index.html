The driving effects disappear well after horizon crossing since gravitational potential decays. The boost is reduced because of the existence of neutrinos.

https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1086/319449

  1. CMB power spectrum中可以提取四个重要的量
    • the position of the first peak $\ell_1$
    • the height of the first peak relative to the power at $\ell_{10}$: $H_1\equiv (\Delta T_{\ell_1}/\Delta T_{10})^2$,$\Delta T_\ell^2\equiv \ell(\ell+1)C_\ell/2\pi$
    • the height of the second peak relative to the first
    • height of the third peak relative to the first
  2. The locations of the second and the third are set by their harmonic relation to the first peak
  3. $\ell_1$ is determined by the ratio of the comoving angular-diameter distance to the last scattering and the sound horizon at that epoch. Thus depends on geometry geometry and sound-wave dynamics through $\Omega_m+\Omega_\Lambda,h,\Omega_m,w,\Omega_bh^2$ in decreasing order of importance

$$
l_A \approx 172 d\left(\frac{z_*}{10^3}\right)^{1 / 2}\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{R_*}} \ln \frac{\sqrt{1+R_*}+\sqrt{R_*+r_* R_*}}{1+\sqrt{r_* R_*}}\right)^{-1},
$$

  1. The effect of tilt on $\ell_1$ is small.

  2. For a Ñat geometry, the position of the first peak is strongly correlated with the age of the universe.

  3. The $H_2$ statistic, the ratio of the heights of the second
    peak to the first, mainly depends on the tilt parameter and
    the baryon abundance. This combination is insensitive to
    reionization, the presence of tensor modes, or any effects
    that are confined to the lowest multipoles.

Scaling relations

IDEALIZED

Considering a idealized picture of photon-baryon fliud before recombination that neglects dissipation and time variation in sound speed and gravitational driving forces. The effective temperature perturbation is then
$$
\Delta T\left(\eta_*, k\right)=\left[\Delta T(0, k)+R_* \Psi\right] \cos \left(k s_\right)-R_ \Psi
$$
Baryons modulate the amplitude of the oscillation by shifting the zero point, leading to asymmetry between odd and even peaks.

In this idealized condition, peaks in angular power spectrum are at $\ell_m=m\ell_A$,
$$
l_A \equiv \pi D/s_\approx 172 d\left(\frac{z_}{10^3}\right)^{1 / 2}\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{R_*}} \ln \frac{\sqrt{1+R_*}+\sqrt{R_*+r_* R_*}}{1+\sqrt{r_* R_*}}\right)^{-1},
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
r_* & \equiv \rho_r\left(z_\right) / \rho_m\left(z_\right)=0.042 \omega_m^{-1}\left(z_ / 10^3\right), \
R_ & \equiv 3 \rho_b\left(z_*\right) / 4 \rho_\gamma\left(z_\right)=30 \omega_b\left(z_ / 10^3\right)^{-1}
\end{aligned}
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
& z_* \approx 1008\left(1+0.00124 \omega_b^{-0.74}\right)\left(1+c_1 \omega_m^{c_2}\right) \
& c_1=0.0783 \omega_b^{-0.24}\left(1+39.5 \omega_b^{0.76}\right)^{-1} \
& c_2=0.56\left(1+21.1 \omega_b^{1.8}\right)^{-1}
\end{aligned}
$$

DRIVING and DISSIPATIVE

Driving effect comes from the decay of the gravitational potential in the radiation-dominated epoch, which enhances the oscillations and leads to an increase in power of a factore of 20 for $\ell>\ell_{\rm eq}$

$$
l_{\mathrm{eq}} \equiv\left(2 \Omega_m H_0^2 z_{\mathrm{eq}}\right)^{1 / 2} D \approx 438 d \omega_m^{1 / 2} .
$$

It also introduces a phase shift to the oscillations
$$
l_m=l_A(m-\phi), \quad \phi \approx 0.267\left(\frac{r_*}{0.3}\right)^{0.1} .
$$

Radiation driving reduces the baryon drag effect by reducing the depth of the potential wells at $z_*$
