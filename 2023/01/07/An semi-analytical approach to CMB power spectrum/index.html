Modern cosmology + Hu1995a Anisotroies in the CMB:an analytic approach

Tight coupling solutions

To first order (no damping)
$$
\ddot \Theta_0+\frac{\dot a}{a}\frac{R}{1+R}\dot \Theta_0+k^2c_s^2\Theta_0=F(\eta)\
F(\eta)=-\ddot \Phi-\frac{\dot a}{a}\frac{R}{1+R}\dot\Phi-\frac{k^2}{3}\Psi
$$
Aside from expansion damping, there are three major tight coupling evolutionary effects

  • $\ddot \Phi$, the ISW effect on $\ddot \Theta_0$, dominates at superhorizon scales $k\eta\ll 1$;
  • $k^2\Psi$, the gravitational infall, important near the horizon scale $k\eta\sim 1$;
  • $k^2c_s^2\Theta_0$, the photon pressure which cannot be neglected inside the sound horizon $kr_s\gtrsim 1$

The homogeneous $F(\eta)=0$ equation can be solved by WKB method. The WKB approximation is valid for all modes are in the oscillating regime by last scatter, i.e. that smaller than the sound horizon at recombination.

$s_1=(1+R)^{-1/4}\cos kr_S,s_2=(1+R)^{-1/4}\sin kr_S$

Employing the Green’s method, the undamped solution is
$$
\hat \Theta_0(\eta)=C_1s_1(\eta)+C_2s_2(\eta)+\int _0^\eta\frac{s_1(\eta’)s_2(\eta)-s_1(\eta)s_2(\eta’)}{s_1(\eta’)\dot s_2(\eta’)-\dot s_1(\eta’)s_2(\eta’)}F(\eta’)d\eta’
$$

$$
s_1(\eta’)s_2(\eta)-s_1(\eta)s_2(\eta’)=(1+R(\eta))^{-1/4}(1+R(\eta’))^{-1/4}\sin(kr_s(\eta)-kr_s(\eta’))
\
s_1(\eta’)\dot s_2(\eta’)-\dot s_1(\eta’)s_2(\eta’)=\frac{k}{\sqrt{3}}\frac{1}{1+R(\eta’)}
$$

Match them with initial condition
$$
\Rightarrow (1+R(\eta))^{1/4}\hat\Theta_0(\eta)=\Theta_0\cos kr_s(\eta)+\frac{\sqrt{3}}{k}[\dot \Theta_0(0)+\frac{1}{4}\dot R(0)\Theta_0(0)]\sin kr_s(\eta)+\frac{\sqrt{3}}{k}\int_0^\eta d\eta’[1+R(\eta’)]^{3/4}\sin[kr_s(\eta)-kr_s(\eta’)]F(\eta’)
$$

By using $\dot \Theta_0+\frac{k}{3}\Theta_1=-\dot\Phi\rightarrow k\Theta_1=-3(\dot \Theta_0+\dot \Phi)$ we can then have the function for $\Theta_1$.
