Confronting interacting dark radiation scenarios with cosmological data

  • Fluid-like radiation: always interacting. Perturbations in a relativistic fluid are completely characterized by the equation of state $w=P/\rho$ and sound speed $c_s=\delta p/\delta \rho$. For a tightly coupled relativistic fluid, $w=c_s^2=c^2/3$. The fluid anisotropic stress and all higher moments vanish
  • Fluid perturbations propagate at $c_s=c/\sqrt{3}$ while perturbations in relativistic free-streaming particles propagate at $c$. ( This is why free-streaming neutrino induces phase shift on CMB.)
  • Free-streaming particles have significant anisotropic stress and full Boltzmann hierarchy is required rather than just the continuity equation. (The “anisotropic stress” will finally reduce the power of CMB)

Fig.3: Decoupling means radiations are interacting at early time and then free-streaming, like self-interacting neutrinos.

For wavenumber $k=1 /{\rm Mpc}$, the horizon-crossing transition occurs at $c_sk\tau\sim 1$, ($k\tau\sim \sqrt{3}$) I think here should be sound horizon not horizon. which corresponds to $z\sim 10^5$, deep in the radiation-dominated era.

  • Horizon crossing behavior: “Fluid” oscillates with larger amplitude (Yellow curve) while “Free-streaming” is highly suppressed at late time ( (b)gray).
  • If the decoupling transition is complete prior to horizon crossing, then the DR perturbations match the free-streaming and fluid-like cases exactly. (Not shown in figures.)
  • In (b), curves decouple instantaneously show a nearly immediate transition between following fluid and free-streaming. For example, $z_{\rm dec}=10^5$ case, it follows “fluid” at early time and then follows “free-streaming”
  • Similar characteristics in “Decoupling” case panel (a) $G_{\rm eff}=0.021$ while this slower transition makes the perturbation deviate from the fluid-like curve at an earlier time and take longer time to approach the free-streaming solution.
  • Fluid DR will undergo acoustic oscillations along with the photon-baryon fluid (see Fig. 3), enhancing
    the amplitude of acoustic oscillations in photon-baryon fluid, and therefore CMB temperature and polarization power spectra. This can also be explained as interacting DR->no anistropic stress->larger graviational potential-> more decay power into perturbations
  • Free-streaming particles do not participate in acoustic oscillations, and thereby suppress the amplitude of
    oscillations in CMB power spectra. But induce phase shift. Free-streaming means reducation in power and phase shift
  • For DR that transitions between fluid-like and free-streaming during the radiation dominated era, the observables
    acquire a scale-dependence. For instance, photon perturbations that cross the horizon while the DR is fluid-like ($k\gg 1/(c_s\tau_{\rm dec})$) will have larger amplitude oscillations and no phase shift (easy to see in (b) the first peak.) While those that cross when the species is free-streaming ($k\ll 1/(c_s\tau_{\rm dec})$) will have reduced oscillation amplitude and a phase shift (panel(b) gray and red curves.)

Appendix

The Neutrino Puzzle: Anomalies, Interactions, and Cosmological Tensions

$k=10,0.3,10^{-3}$ correspond to modes enter the horizon before $z_{\rm dec}$ , during $z_{\rm dec}$ and after, respectively. $z_{\rm dec}\simeq z_{\rm eq}$

  • $d_{\rm cdm}\equiv \delta_{\rm cdm}-3\phi$, at late times $d_{\rm cdm}$ is nearly equal to $\delta_c$ and thus it’s a useful quantity to understand the structure of the matter power spectrum at $z=0$.
  • Remember that strongly coupled neutrinos doesn’t contribute to anistropic stress while free-streaming neutrinos with anistropic stress will suppress the gravitational potential. Thus potential in SInu has a larger value before horizon crossing compared to LCDM in up-left panel.

$k=10$

  • When modes enter the horzion during the radiation-dominated era, gravitational potential $\psi$ decays in an oscillatory fashion. And $\psi$ in SI case has a larger oscillatory amplitude compared with LCDM. (This also means a slower decay of $\psi$ in SI case compared with LCDM.) This then increases the amplitude of dark matter fluctuations at horizon entry in bottom-right panel.
  • The slower decay of the potential in SI reduces the horzion-entry boost that dark matter fluctuations experience compared to LCDM, leading to a net damping in CDM perturbation amplitude in bottom-right panel.

$k=0.3$

  • For modes entering the horizon at the time of neutrino decoupling, the potential begins decaying from its larger value with $R_\nu = 0$ but rapidly locks into its standard ΛCDM evolution due to the onset of neutrino freestreaming. This case thus displays the quickest damping of the potential after horizon entry, which leads to a net boost of dark matter fluctuations as compared to LCDM (in bottom-right panel).

$k=10^-3$

  • modes entering the horizon well-after the onset of neutrino free-streaming behave exactly like their ΛCDM counterparts .

Overall, the matter power spectrum at $z=0$ should exhibit these features:

  • For larger wavenumber (small scale), since CDM perturbations are damped, the matter power spectrum should be suppressed
  • For medium wavenumber (scales entering the horizon at the onset of neutrino free-streaming), since CDM experiences anet boost, there should be a bump in matter power spectrum
  • For small wavenumber (larger scale), should be the same as LCDM.
